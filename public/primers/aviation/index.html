Aviation Industry

Edition 2025–26 Geography — India

India operates the world's third-largest domestic air-travel market1 — the movement of passengers and cargo by airlines, enabled by a supporting web of airports, MRO and ground services. A handful of carriers dominate a fast-consolidating, capital-intensive sector now riding a record fleet order-book and a post-pandemic travel surge.

Industry at a Glance

Definition, players & market structure
Overview

The aviation industry covers domestic & international air transport of passengers and cargo operated by airlines, enabled by airports, MRO and ground services. India ranks 3rd globally in domestic air-traffic handled.

Leading Players
IndiGo Air India Air India Express Akasa Air SpiceJet Vistara* Go First* AIX Connect

*Vistara fully merged into Air India (Nov 2024); Go First grounded since 2023, now in liquidation; AIX Connect merged into Air India Express (Oct 2024).

Competitive & Macro Forces

Porter's Five Forces & PESTEL
Porter's Five Forces
Supplier PowerHigh

Oligopolies in aircraft (Boeing/Airbus) & engine markets.

Buyer PowerModerate

Low switching costs & an undifferentiated product.

Threat of New EntrantsLow

High capital, slot & regulatory entry barriers.

Threat of SubstitutesModerate

Fast trains (Vande Bharat) undercut short-haul routes.

Competitive RivalryHigh

~15 operators, undifferentiated service, recurrent price wars.

Outlook

Trends, challenges & recent developments
Trends
  • New & regional routes
  • Sector consolidation
  • Sustainability & SAF
  • Digitalization
  • Passenger experience
Challenges
  • Price sensitivity
  • Traffic & slot congestion
  • Environmental concerns
  • Fuel-price volatility
  • Pilot shortage & retention
Recent Developments & Policy
  • Mega mergers — Air India–Vistara & Air India Express–AIX Connect consolidate private aviation.
  • Fleet boom — Record Boeing & Airbus orders; 1,600+ aircraft on firm order (IndiGo, Air India, Akasa).
  • SAF targets — 1% SAF blending by 2027, rising to 5% by 2030, applied first to international flights; no domestic mandate notified yet.
  • UDAN 5.1 — MCA scheme adds helicopter routes; 50+ landing sites revived for tier-2/3 connectivity.
  • Airport push — New airports at Noida & Navi Mumbai; expansions in Goa, Patna, Jodhpur.
  • Digital travel — Digi Yatra, contactless check-in & QR-code boarding streamline the journey.
  • Akasa Air — Newer entrant managing a skilled-commander shortage & financial uncertainty.
  • Air India — Consolidating subsidiaries into two core airlines (full-service + low-cost).
