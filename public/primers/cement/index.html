Industry at a Glance

Definition, players & market structure
Overview

The cement industry covers the production, distribution & sale of cement. India is the world's #2 producer, with over 8% of global installed capacity. A bulky, freight-intensive product confines competition to regional markets.

Key Players
UltraTech Adani (Ambuja · ACC) Shree Cement Dalmia Bharat JK Cement JSW Cement Birla Corp. Nuvoco Vistas

Top 5 players hold ~60% national share (up from ~54% in 2023); regional oligopolies exist across markets.

Competitive & Macro Forces

Porter's Five Forces & PESTEL
Porter's Five Forces
Supplier PowerMedium

Govt controls coal & limestone licensing; players backward-integrate.

Buyer PowerLow

Large players retain pricing power; only institutional buyers negotiate.

Threat of New EntrantsModerate

High capex, mines/clearances & limestone access act as barriers.

Threat of SubstitutesLow

Few practical/economic alternatives; low demand elasticity.

Competitive RivalryModerate

Oligopolistic; homogenous product, low control over pricing.

Outlook

Growth drivers, trends & challenges
Growth Drivers
  • High-quality limestone deposits throughout the country
  • Sustained govt infra outlay + PMAY / urban housing support demand
  • Industry adding ~30–45 MTPA annually; utilisation ~65–70%
Trends
  • Cement blending
  • Affordable housing
  • Capacity expansion
  • M&As
  • Carbon neutrality
Challenges
  • Excess capacity, low demand
  • Coal supply & price volatility
  • Freight / rail-rake constraints
  • Land acquisition
  • Environmental concerns
Prevalent Business Models
  • Integrated (mine → clinker → grinding) — structurally lowest cost; heavy capex & compliance
  • Clinker + satellite grinding — flexibility to chase demand; transfer-pricing & clinker availability are key
  • Grinding only — near ports/consumption clusters; asset-light, margin-sensitive to clinker prices
  • Go-to-market — strong trade brand & dealer programs vs institutional bulk (RMC/infra)
  • Adjacencies — RMC, white cement & putty, building-materials retail
Industry-Specific Notes
  • Bulky material — high handling & freight costs keep plants close to consumers
  • Regional markets — transport costs create protected regional oligopolies
  • Retail = push market — layman doesn't differentiate brands; masons & shopkeepers are lead influencers
  • Size & spread — scale enables supplier leverage & lower freight, building a bigger brand
06

Recent Developments & Policy

M&A, regulation & sector news
  • Adani's ~$6.5Bn buyout — Holcim's stake in Ambuja & ACC (~$10.5Bn incl. open offer); the largest M&A in Indian infra/materials.
  • Synergies & M&As — Adani–Ambuja input-utilities synergies; UltraTech, NTPC input-utility tie-ups.
  • GST cut done — Cement GST cut 28%→18%, effective 22 Sep 2025 (GST 2.0); ICRA sees an FY26 operating-profit lift, supporting selective price hikes.
  • EU CBAM — Moves to the definitive (paid) phase in 2026, covering cement; Indian EU exports face embedded-carbon pricing.
  • SHAKTI coal-linkage — Revised 2025 policy notified; coal reforms continue, key to kiln-fuel economics.
  • Policy tailwinds — NIP, NMP, PMAY (urban/gramin), Smart Cities & AMRUT continue to grow cement demand.
