FMCG Industry

Edition 2025–26 Geography — India

Fast-moving consumer goods1 — the production, distribution & sale of frequently-purchased, non-durable products: food & beverages, healthcare and household & personal care. India's FMCG market grew 16% in FY21, its fastest in nine years, and ranks among the world's five largest — still ~80% routed through kirana stores, even as quick commerce rewrites the last mile.

Competitive & Macro Forces

Porter's Five Forces & PESTEL
Porter's Five Forces
Supplier PowerLow

Fragmented suppliers; big firms control pricing, multiple options, low switching cost.

Buyer PowerHigh

Many options, very price-sensitive; volume negotiation reduces margins.

Threat of New EntrantsMedium

Easy digital access, but strong brands, distribution scale & regulation are barriers.

Threat of SubstitutesHigh

Unbranded, private labels & small packs widely available; narrow differentiation.

Competitive RivalryHigh

Very fragmented; intense price wars, low brand loyalty, fight for shelf space.

PESTEL Analysis
P
Political — PLI schemes & relaxed FDI (100% single-brand, 51% multi-brand); GST & trade policy.
E
Economic — Rising income lifts demand (esp. rural/semi-urban); inflation & input volatility hurt margins.
S
Social — Urbanization & youth drive convenience; health-focused, clean-label & on-the-go demand rising.
T
Technological — E/Q-commerce & D2C disrupt; AI/ML & IoT for demand prediction; QR traceability.
E
Environmental — Eco-packaging, carbon & waste reduction; ESG & CSR growing in brand relevance.
L
Legal — Consumer Protection Act 2019, FSSAI, Drugs & Cosmetics Act 1940; labelling & ad norms.

Outlook

Growth, trends, challenges & business models
Growth Drivers
  • Rising incomes, rural development & MSME support
  • E-commerce ≈ 17% of sales among affluent consumers
  • Growing urbanisation & changing lifestyles lift packaged-goods demand
Trends
  • Health & wellness
  • E-commerce rise
  • Sustainability
  • Convenience culture
  • ONDC
Challenges
  • Rising input costs
  • Weak urban demand
  • Channel conflicts (q-com)
  • Rural penetration
  • Illicit trade & compliance
Prevalent Business Models
  • Traditional — kirana stores & offline networks (~80% of sales, esp. rural)
  • E-commerce & D2C — fast-growing, convenient, direct consumer insight
  • Data-driven — premium services, rewards & analytics to boost loyalty
  • Omnichannel & private label — seamless on/offline; retailers' own brands
  • Subscription & sustainable sourcing — curated boxes; eco-conscious products
Industry-Specific Notes
  • Urban–rural divide — rural outperformed urban in Q4 FY25; rural basket up 60% (2022–24)
  • Modern trade grew 19.5%; food & non-food categories at 8.7%
  • Quick commerce — expected US$25–55B by 2030, led by high-frequency users
  • Pet-food market — FMCG firms eye growth as pet ownership & incomes rise

Recent Developments & Policy

M&A, regulation & sector news
  • FDI liberalised — GoI approved 100% FDI in single-brand & 51% in multi-brand retail to promote FMCG.
  • Key M&As — HUL–GSK CH; Tata Digital–BigBasket; Marico–Beardo; Dabur–Badshah Masala.
  • Reliance's entry — Deep pockets & vast network pose a significant competitive threat.
  • Kwality Wall's — Magnum Ice Cream HoldCo to acquire 61.9% from the Unilever Group.
  • Campa goes global — Reliance launches Campa in Sri Lanka with Elephant House Beverages.
  • Regulatory headwinds — E-com deep-discounting rules & quality standards (honey adulteration).
  • Q-commerce & eB2B — Reshaping traditional distribution; limited long-term trust in 3P platforms.
  • AI & tech adoption — SFA & DMS tools boost sales, targeting & supply-chain efficiency.
